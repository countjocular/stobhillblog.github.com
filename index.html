So, what exactly is it we're building?

We're trying to build an interactive 3d game, based on Edwin Morgan's poem Stobhill. The poem tells the story of a young woman who has had an abortion. It's told from the point of view of several different characters, each of whom provides an account in the form of a seperate monologued poem.

We want to explore new ways of presenting literature and story. We're going to be splitting the poem up into pieces, and scattering the pieces throughout our 3d space. The player will encounter the poem over the course of the game. The order in which the poem is discovered is (partially) dependant on the player.

The plot we've come up with is this: you play the role of a young girl, who has been dared by her friends to explore the abandoned Stobhill hospital building. Your only companion is your old, beloved teddy bear - who begins to speak to you once you enter the building. It's spooky, slightly unreal, and full of psycological drama.

As you wander around the hospital, you'll encounter different snippets of Morgan's poem. Gradually, the full story will build itself.
